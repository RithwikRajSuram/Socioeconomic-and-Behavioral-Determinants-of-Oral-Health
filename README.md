# Socioeconomic and Behavioral Determinants of Oral Health (NHANES 2013–2018)

📌 Overview
This capstone project analyzes how income, education, and smoking status influence oral health disparities among U.S. adults using NHANES 2013–2018 data.

🎯 Objective
To quantify the independent effects of socioeconomic and behavioral factors on:
Untreated dental caries (binary outcome)
Missing teeth (count outcome)

📊 Data Source
National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) 2013–2018
~25,000 U.S. adults aged ≥18

🧠 Methods
Data cleaning and harmonization across 3 survey cycles
χ² tests and t-tests for bivariate associations
Logistic regression (untreated caries)
Linear regression (missing teeth)
Adjusted for age, sex, race/ethnicity, and insurance
Listwise deletion for missing values

📈 Key Findings
Untreated caries: 34% (low-income) vs 15% (high-income)
Income-to-poverty ratio OR: 0.74 (95% CI: 0.61–0.89)
Education (College vs HS) OR: 0.69
Smoking OR: 1.95
Model explanatory power: R² ≈ 0.28

💡 Public Health Implication
Socioeconomic and behavioral determinants significantly influence oral health disparities, supporting expansion of preventive care and smoking cessation programs.
